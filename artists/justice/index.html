Artist · Electro wave

Justice

Justice brought a new, aggressive energy to French electronic music in the 2000s. Their sound combined distorted basslines, rock aesthetics and a strong sense of drama that resonated far beyond traditional club scenes.

Crunch, distortion and drama

Where French Touch leaned towards smoothness and groove, Justice leaned towards crunch and shock. Their debut album Cross sounded like a compressed wall of sound, yet every track was carefully structured and full of hooks, making it as memorable as it was noisy.

Signature elements

  • Gritty, distorted bass and synth lines.
  • Heavy side-chain compression and maximalist mixes.
  • References to rock, metal and classical music in both sound and visuals.
  • Strong, iconic logo and stage design contributing to their mythos.

Key releases

  • † (Cross) (2007)
  • Audio, Video, Disco (2011)
  • Woman (2016)

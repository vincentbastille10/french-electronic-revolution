Artist · Pioneer

Jean-Michel Jarre

Jean-Michel Jarre is one of the earliest and most influential voices in French electronic music. With albums like Oxygène and Équinoxe, he transformed synthesizers into tools for widescreen, contemplative soundscapes.

From experimental to monumental

Jarre’s music bridges experimental electronic sound design and accessible, melodic composition. His large-scale outdoor concerts — often staged in front of monuments, skylines or industrial structures — turned electronic music into a public spectacle, long before EDM festivals existed.

Signature elements

  • Long, evolving synth lines and arpeggios.
  • Strong use of stereo image and spatialisation.
  • Slow-moving harmonies that feel both futuristic and nostalgic.
  • A visual language built around light, lasers and architecture.

Key releases

  • Oxygène (1976)
  • Équinoxe (1978)
  • Les Concerts en Chine (1982)

Jarre’s legacy can be heard across the entire history of French electronic music, from ambient and film scores to the more cinematic wave of the 2010s.
